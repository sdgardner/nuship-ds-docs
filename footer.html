Footer — use when:
  • Anchoring the bottom of every page in the product
  • Surfacing legal, accessibility, and help links in a consistent place
  • The link rail wraps to a second line on narrower viewports

Usage Guidelines

Organize links by purpose
  • Group related links into clear columns with descriptive section headings
  • Limit each column to 5–8 links so the footer remains scannable
Include legal & compliance
  • Surface Privacy, Terms, and Cookie policy links — these are expected here
  • Show copyright with the current year and brand entity
Reinforce brand & contact
  • Include the brand mark and a short value statement when space allows
  • Provide contact channels and social links if they exist
Be responsive
  • Stack columns into a single column on mobile breakpoints
  • Maintain adequate tap targets (44×44 px) for all links
Overload with links
  • Don't use the footer as a sitemap of every page
  • Don't add more than 4 columns of links on desktop
Hide critical information
  • Don't bury Privacy or Terms — they must remain visible from the footer
  • Don't omit copyright, contact, or compliance markers required for your region
Replace primary navigation
  • Don't move primary navigation into the footer — top-level routes belong in the Header
  • Don't repeat the entire top nav verbatim; choose a focused subset
Break the layout
  • Don't allow link labels to wrap mid-word or overflow their column
  • Don't change the footer position based on scroll — keep it at the end of the document

Accessibility

  • Landmark: Wrap in <footer role="contentinfo"> so assistive tech can jump to it directly.
  • Keyboard: Every link must be reachable in tab order with a visible focus ring.
  • Contrast: Copyright text (#4B5563 on white) clears WCAG AA at 7:1; link blue (#016692) clears AA at 4.5:1.
  • Wrap behaviour: The link rail wraps gracefully on narrow viewports — no horizontal scroll.
